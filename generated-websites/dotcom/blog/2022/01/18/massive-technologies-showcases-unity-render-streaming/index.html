    In early September 2021, hubraum launched their latest 10 week program for startups and developers focused around iOS and Augmented Reality Innovation. At the kickoff event, Vasanth Mohan, Developer Advocate at MobiledgeX, gave a brief presentation on the advantages of leveraging the MobiledgeX platform for telco edge computing, especially as Deutsche Telekom has a nationwide public edge footprint across Germany that developers can leverage for their applications. During the course of the program, MobiledgeX worked with a handful of startups to highlight how they can take advantage of the resources. One such startup, Massive Technologies, is developing an iOS app that is capable of converting an audio file into an Augmented Reality Piano Concert. Some of Massive Technologies’ apps are downloadable today on the Apple App Store, where they were chosen by Apple as App Store Best of 2017 in the AR category:

    • AR Pianist - 3D Piano Concerts

    • Piano 3D - Real AR Piano App

    As part of the hubraum iOS AR Innovation program, Massive Technologies was able to leverage the capabilities of telco edge computing to improve the user experience and graphical fidelity of the AR Concert. By leveraging 5G and edge computing on MobiledgeX, Massive Technologies was able to incorporate Unity’s Open Source Render Streaming SDK into their app, which enables them to run a PC version of their app on an edge computing server and stream the video feed to an iPhone or iPad. 

    By running the app on an edge server with a dedicated NVIDIA RTX GPU, they are able to render concerts in much higher fidelity by leveraging Real Time Ray Tracing, a computationally intensive algorithm that would otherwise exhaust smartphone resources. Moreover, to really highlight the difference between device and remote server rendering with Real Time Ray Tracing, Massive Technology developed a demo wherein you can instantly transition from a device rendered AR concert to a edge server rendered AR concert

    As you can see in this comparison video, by leveraging Real Time Ray Tracing, the realism is dramatically increased due to improved reflections, lighting conditions, and materials. In this case, the edge latency ensures that the user experience remains unchanged by making the delay between the movement of the device and visual display nearly unnoticeable. As this app continues to be developed, Massive Technologies can leverage the MobiledgeX platform in order to:

    1. Incorporate MobiledgeX Unity / iOS SDKs into their client app to determine the most optimal edge server relative to the device, based on location and cellular connectivity

    2. Leverage MobiledgeX Auto-Provisioning policies to dynamically create edge servers on demand of their users.

    MobiledgeX is proud to have partnered with hubraum and Massive Technologies to support these unique use cases and highlight the advantages of edge computing in tangible ways.  If you are a startup that has a use case that could benefit from edge computing, please head to our MobiledgeX Developer Portal to learn how you can get started building on the Telco Edge.
