    Mawari: Remote Graphics Rendering for AR

    Mawari is developing an Augmented Reality solution that leverages edge computing by streaming high quality 3D content to devices like smartphones over 5G. This process, known as remote rendering, enables you to view content that is not possible to render using the built-in computation of the mobile device. By leveraging Nvidia GPUs that are deployed on telco edge computing servers, Mawari is able to utilize the low latency of the network and hardware acceleration of GPUs to create a user experience that is almost indistinguishable from device based rendering.

    In collaboration with Deutsche Telekom and KDDI, Mawari was able to deploy their remote rendering solution through the MobiledgeX platform in both operator regions. This collaboration was done as a GSMA TEC trial and they released a demonstration video of the technology at MWC LA, called Bloom City:

    KDDI has also deployed the Mawari solution along with other ISV (Sturfee) as part of their OpenAPI initiative on the MobiledgeX Platform. This offering is exposed to developers as a Digital Human as a Service API. Developers located in Japan have the opportunity to start working with these APIs to create products that can leverage the Digital Human showcased in the Bloom City project.

    KDDI XR Open APIs for Developers

    For more information on the Bloom City demonstration, you can also review our Panel discussion that Vasanth Mohan, Lead Developer Advocate, gave at the Augmented World Expo conference.

    Technical Requirements

    • Devices : Augmented Reality devices like nReal / AR Smartphones (iOS  & Android)

    • Edge Requirements : Windows Virtual Machines, GPUs (Nvidia Ampere Series recommended)

    • SDK: Unity
